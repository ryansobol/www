"If you're tired of compromising, check out Ryan Sobol."

I've been a professional software developer for the past 10 years. And I hate using tools that are incomplete and unintuitive.

That's why the software I create is balanced, insightful and compassionate. In my experience, this requires building small, effective teams who:

  • Put the user's experience first,
  • Collaborate using state-of-the-art tools,
  • Adhere to a minimal process,
  • Operate within and outside their comfort zone,
  • Reduce interruptions to the working flow,
  • And feel responsible for the project's outcome.

I believe most software teams want to build great products. But sometimes they need a little help — either starting from scratch, finishing on time, or getting back on track.

If your team feels overwhelmed, I can help. Whatever the state of your project, even if every detail isn't nailed down, my remarkably straightforward approach will work.

  1. Assemble as a team
  2. Decide what's next
  3. Get to work
  4. Communicate as needed
  5. Deliver only what's next
  6. Show it off
  7. Review the feedback
  8. Repeat until finished
